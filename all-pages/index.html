Topic documents

Each of these runs through the whole life of a service, from before it exists to after it is switched off. Most people need two or three of them at a time, and the phase pages tell you which ones matter where you are. They are in four groups, by what you are trying to do.

1. Getting it funded, bought, and staffed

Almost every service is bought or contracted rather than built in-house, so these three decide most of what follows.

FundingProcurementTeam capability

2. Duties you have to meet

Set by law, policy, or directive. Every service has to meet them.

AccessibilitySecurityPrivacyData stewardshipEthics and bias

3. The people you serve

Whether the service works, whether it fits the wider journey a person is on, and whether anyone switches to it.

User researchJoined-up deliveryChange management

4. Running it, year after year

Live is the longest phase by a wide margin, and this is the work that fills it.

Releasing changesMonitoring and instrumentationBacklogDependencies and standards

New to all of this?

  1. Read the home page. It says what the guide is and who it is for.
  2. Find your phase: Create if the service does not exist yet, Live if it is running, Sunset if it is being retired or replaced.
  3. Open the sub-phase that matches where you are, and work from there.
  4. Pull in the topic documents as the pages point you to them.
