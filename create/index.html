How the Create phase works

Where this fits

CreateLiveSunset
DiscoveryAlphaBeta

Where a service goes from a problem to a working first version in users' hands. The team:

  • works out what the problem actually is
  • decides whether to reuse, buy, or build
  • gets the service funded and approved
  • delivers the first version

Most of what shapes the service is decided here.

Create is the project part of a service's life: the one stretch with a clear finish line. That finish is launch, the day the service goes live.

It runs from the first idea to launch. In between, a team works out whether the service should exist at all, what it should be, and how to pay for it and stand it up. Create is delivered in three sub-phases, Discovery, Alpha, and Beta, from learning the need to a real service ready to launch.

After launch the work does not end. It changes: the service stops being a project and becomes a product that has to be run and improved for years, which is Live.

The work of Create

Create is three kinds of work, and it ends on the day the service goes live.

These are project decisions, made to get the service built. The business case is made, the money for the build is committed, and the design is signed off. After launch those same checks start coming round again, every release and every year, and that recurring rhythm is what makes Live different.

Create in three sub-phases

Create is delivered in three sub-phases, each with its own page. Approval and funding are not settled all at once before they begin. They build up across the sub-phases, and the big commitment, the money and sign-offs for the build, comes before Beta.

Discovery →

work out whether the service is needed, and whether to reuse, buy, or build. Stopping here is a good outcome.

Alpha →

test the riskiest ideas with throwaway prototypes, before committing.

Beta →

stand up the real service and prove it with real users, before launch.

Launch is the crossing into Live: the service goes live and becomes the real one people use, in place of whatever they did before.
